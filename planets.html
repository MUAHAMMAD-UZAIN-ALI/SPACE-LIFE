An Interactive Guide to the Cosmos

Stars

Stars are massive, luminous spheres of plasma, held together by their own gravity. They are the fundamental building blocks of galaxies, producing light and heat through nuclear fusion.

The Sun

Diameter: 1.39 million km

Type: G-type Main-sequence Star

Surface Temperature: 5,500 °C

Location: Center of our Solar System

Planets

Planets are celestial bodies orbiting a star, massive enough to be rounded by their own gravity, and have cleared their neighboring region of smaller objects.

Mercury

Diameter: 4,879 km

Type: Terrestrial

Moons: None

Venus

Diameter: 12,104 km

Type: Terrestrial

Moons: None

Earth

Diameter: 12,742 km

Type: Terrestrial

Moons: 1

Mars

Diameter: 6,779 km

Type: Terrestrial

Moons: 2

Jupiter

Diameter: 139,820 km

Type: Gas Giant

Moons: 95 confirmed

Saturn

Diameter: 116,460 km

Type: Gas Giant

Moons: 146 confirmed

Uranus

Diameter: 50,724 km

Type: Ice Giant

Moons: 27 confirmed

Neptune

Diameter: 49,244 km

Type: Ice Giant

Moons: 14 confirmed

Moons

Moons are natural satellites that orbit planets or other celestial bodies. Our solar system contains a diverse array of moons, from rocky spheres to icy worlds.

Earth's Moon

Orbiting: Earth

Diameter: 3,474 km

Surface: Rocky, cratered

Titan

Orbiting: Saturn

Diameter: 5,150 km

Atmosphere: Dense, nitrogen-rich

Ganymede

Orbiting: Jupiter

Diameter: 5,268 km

Fact: Largest moon in the Solar System

Triton

Orbiting: Neptune

Diameter: 2,707 km

Fact: Orbits in a retrograde direction

Dwarf Planets, Comets & Asteroids

These smaller bodies provide crucial insights into the formation and history of our solar system, from the icy wanderers to the rocky fragments.

Pluto

Diameter: 2,376 km

Type: Dwarf Planet

Moons: 5 confirmed

Halley's Comet

Type: Short-period Comet

Orbital Period: ~76 years

Last Seen: 1986

The Asteroid Belt

Location: Between Mars & Jupiter

Contents: Asteroids & Dwarf Planet Ceres

Fun Fact: Most of its volume is empty space.

Galaxies

Galaxies are vast systems of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter bound together by gravity.

The Milky Way

Type: Barred Spiral Galaxy

Diameter: ~105,700 light-years

Fact: Our home galaxy

Andromeda Galaxy

Type: Spiral Galaxy

Distance: 2.5 million light-years

Fact: The closest major galaxy to the Milky Way

Triangulum Galaxy

Type: Spiral Galaxy

Distance: 3 million light-years

Fact: Third-largest galaxy in the Local Group

Black Holes

Black holes are regions of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing—no particles or even electromagnetic radiation such as light—can escape from it.

Sagittarius A*

Type: Supermassive Black Hole

Location: Center of the Milky Way

Mass: 4 million solar masses

M87 Black Hole

Type: Supermassive Black Hole

Location: Center of Galaxy M87

Fact: First black hole to be imaged

Spacecraft & Probes

Humanity has sent a fleet of robotic explorers across the solar system, providing us with stunning images and invaluable scientific data.

Voyager 1

Mission: Explored Outer Solar System

Status: Interstellar space

Curiosity Rover

Launched: 2011

Mission: Explored Gale Crater on Mars

Status: Active

New Horizons

Launched: 2006

Mission: Pluto & Kuiper Belt Flyby

Status: Active
