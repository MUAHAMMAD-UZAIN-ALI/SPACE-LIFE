Telescope Building History

A timeline of my journey from a beginner to building my own cosmic tools.

A Journey of Discovery

Building your own telescope is a rewarding challenge. Here's a look at my progression and the key milestones along the way.

First Steps (2015)

The First Telescope

My journey began with a simple 4.5-inch Newtonian reflector. I learned the basics of mirror grinding and polishing, a truly hands-on experience that cemented my passion for the hobby.

Advanced Design (2018)

The 8-inch Dobsonian

I graduated to a larger, more complex build. This 8-inch Dobsonian telescope was a massive leap, requiring precision woodworking for the mount and careful alignment of the optics.

Digital Integration (2021)

Automated Go-To Mount

To prepare for astrophotography, I built a custom Go-To mount. This was a deep dive into electronics and programming, allowing me to automatically track and find celestial objects with a press of a button.

Future Projects (2024+)

The 12-inch Truss-Tube

My next project is a portable 12-inch truss-tube Dobsonian. It will combine the best of my previous builds: a large aperture for stunning views and a collapsible design for portability.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best type of telescope for a beginner?

A Dobsonian reflector is often recommended for beginners. It's affordable, easy to set up, and offers a large aperture for great views of deep-sky objects.

How do you take pictures of nebulae and galaxies?

Deep-sky astrophotography requires a camera, a stable equatorial mount to track the stars, and image processing software to stack multiple long-exposure shots.

What is "light pollution" and how can I deal with it?

Light pollution is excess artificial light that obscures faint celestial objects. You can combat it by using light pollution filters or by traveling to a dark-sky location away from cities.
